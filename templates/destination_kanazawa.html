Kanazawa

Kanazawa is a city of timeless elegance, where samurai districts, exquisite gardens, and a legacy of gold leaf artistry reflect a rich cultural heritage.

Kenroku-en is the place to go if you love walking in a garden during every season.

If you want to see geisha perform and see a traditional neighborhood go to Higashi Chaya District.

Also check out Kanazawa Castle and the park that is attached to it. It's a castle from the 16th century and is famous for its vast garden.

Omchi Market is a place to go if you are looking for fresh produce, meat, and seafood.

If you want to feel tradition go to the Tsuzumi-mon Gate. It's made of wood and the design is based on the shirabeo, the cords that are used on drums to hold and tune them. These type of drums are used used in Kanazawa's traditional performing arts.

For a nice walk on a trail through thick forests that leads past a waterfall, you should go to Iozen.

Kanazawa has a lot more museums, shrines and gates to explore. If you have time aloso check out those places!
